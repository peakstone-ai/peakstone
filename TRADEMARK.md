# Peakstone Trademark & Branding Policy

The Peakstone **source code** is open under the Apache License 2.0 ([LICENSE](./LICENSE)). The
Apache license deliberately does **not** grant trademark rights (§6), and this policy spells out how
the **Peakstone name, logo, and the "official" / "verified" designations** may be used. These marks
are reserved by the Peakstone project — they are what keep the benchmark a single, trustworthy,
canonical source even though the code is freely forkable.

## You may, without asking
- Use, modify, and self-host the code under Apache-2.0.
- Truthfully say your project **"is built on Peakstone"**, **"uses Peakstone"**, or **"is
  Peakstone-compatible"** (e.g. emits the Peakstone result-bundle schema).
- Reference Peakstone by name in articles, talks, and comparisons.

## You may not, without written permission
- Name a fork, service, product, or organization **"Peakstone"** or anything confusingly similar.
- Present your instance, leaderboard, or "verified" badge as **the official Peakstone**, or imply
  endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation.
- Use the Peakstone name or logo such that your results could be mistaken for the **canonical
  Peakstone results**.

## What is canonical
The **official Peakstone instance**, the **canonized suites** (versioned), and the **verified result
tier** are operated by the project maintainers. Anyone may run the open corpus and publish results,
but only the official instance issues the canonical leaderboards and the "verified" designation.
